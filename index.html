Understanding the System
Grading Methodology

Each player is evaluated across four independent dimensions, then combined into a single composite overall grade. Grades are expressed as percentile ranks within the player's position group — not raw numbers. A 90 means the player performed in the 90th percentile among all players at their position on the roster. All grades are rescaled to a 0–100 display range for readability.

Production
Production

Absolute output relative to peers. Volume statistics like total tackles, receiving yards, or sacks compared across the position group. Rewards players who produced big numbers in aggregate.

DL: 0.35×Tackles + 0.35×TFL + 0.30×Sacks
WR: Rec Yds + TDs + Catches
Efficiency
Efficiency

Per-game impact metrics. Normalizes for games played, so a player with elite per-snap output scores high here even with limited usage. The truest reflection of quality vs. quantity.

DL: 0.42×TFL/G + 0.38×Sacks/G + 0.20×FF/G
WR: Yds/Rec + Yds/G + TD rate
Physicals
Physicals

Pure size-based grade. Height and weight percentile relative to position group peers. Weights are position-specific: WR grades prioritize height (70%), DL prioritizes weight (60%). Does not reflect athleticism.

WR: 0.70×Height%ile + 0.30×Weight%ile
DL: 0.40×Height%ile + 0.60×Weight%ile
Usage
Usage

A proxy for coaching trust and role significance. Primarily driven by games played (70%), supplemented by involvement rate. High usage + high efficiency = the ideal combination.

0.70×Games Played %ile
+ 0.30×Volume Stat %ile
